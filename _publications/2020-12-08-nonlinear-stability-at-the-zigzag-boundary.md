We investigate the dynamics of roll solutions at the zigzag boundary of the
planar Swift-Hohenberg equation. Linear analysis shows an algebraic decay of
small perturbation with a rate -1/4, instead of the classical diffusive decay
rate -1/2, due to the degeneracy of the quadratic term of the continuation of
the translational mode of the linearized operator in the Bloch-Fourier spaces.
The proof is based on a decomposition of the neutral mode and the faster
decaying modes in the Bloch-Fourier space, and a fixed-point argument,
demonstrating the irrelevancy of the nonlinear terms.
